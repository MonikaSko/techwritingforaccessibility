Color contrast

This section provides information about how and why we should use colors in the documentation.

When we talk about what color and how to use it in our documentation, we talk about three different things:

  • Hue (red, green, blue)

  • Saturation (pastel, bold)

  • Brightness (closer to white, closer to black)

If you make any color hue or saturation changes, they may not be visible to readers who have low vision or are color-blind. They can also entirely disappear when viewed on devices with so-called digital wellness settings (grayscale).

It is important to remember that the only thing visible to all users is brightness.

How to work with color in your documentation?

  • Take color contrast guidelines into consideration.

  • Make sure the colors you use have a lot of contrast against their background (both for fonts and images). No combination of white and light gray is allowed.

  • Add a text alternative for very complicated charts and graphic elements with color.

  • If possible, add a secondary indicator when needed (for example, underline, bold, or add color background to all links).

  • Do not rely on color alone to differentiate elements (e.g., different chart parts or links).

Why we should pay attention to how we add data to the visuals?

Using a secondary indicator to associate labels with chart elements means the color of the bar or segment no longer matters. In this case, the labels have been placed inside outlined segments. When we want to use line charts, using different dash patterns or dot shapes could accomplish the same.

 

Test for accessibility

Use tools

To test your color choices, you can easily use automatic checkers like Color Contrast Analyser, Free desktop application for color, or WAVE.

or

You can also consult your friendly neighborhood UX designer or developer and ask if:

  • all large text (24px regular or 19px bold) have at least a 3:1 contrast ratio

  • all small text has at least a 4:5:1 contrast ratio

 

To check applicable WCAG Success Criteria, go to Color is not the only visual means of conveying information and Text and images of text have a contrast ratio of 4.5:1.
