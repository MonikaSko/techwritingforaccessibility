Multimedia

This section shows tips and tricks for accessible multimedia.

Tutorials or instructional videos are an important part of documentation or help center.

It is worth mentioning that WCAG requires pre-recorded video to be captioned at A, while live video requires captioning at AA standard.

It is important to remember that all video controls should be accessible via the keyboard.

Depending on what kind of content you are adding to your instructions, you should:

  • Provide synchronized captions for audio and visual content (tutorials, how-to's),

  • Provide video transcripts or captions for visual content,

  • Provide a transcript for audio-only content (podcasts),

  • Make sure that you can easily translate captions into other languages.

 

Why are they important?

Providing transcripts and captions may help people who:

  • Use English/Polish as a second language

  • Can’t hear well

  • Can’t hear at all

  • Can’t use sounds in the room they are in

  • Can’t watch flashy screens (transcript)

  • Are sensitive to sound

Captions:

Using captions is very easy and comfortable. They can be stopped at any time, closed or open, which gives user a lot of time to understand the content and re-read it if necessary. Depending on the video and its purpose, it should include:

  • Names

  • Sounds

  • Descriptions

When adding captions to our video, it is crucial to remember about color contrast and how it works for people with various disabilities.

To learn more about using color in your documentation, go to the Color Contrast section.

When creating captions, remember that time stamp should also be available to screen readers.

Transcription:

Transcription ensures any information is conveyed also in written form. You can do it by:

  • Creating script for the video, where the narrator describes all visual information

  • Creating a transcription for audio content

  • Adding a separate audio track that describes the visual content (request only)
