What are screen readers?

This section provides information about some of the screen readers available on the market.

Screen readers (SR) are a form of assistive technology (AT) that help convey what can be seen on a screen via non-visual means, like text-to-speech, sound icons, or a braille device. They do this by applying various techniques, such as interacting with dedicated accessibility APIs, using multiple operating system features (like inter-process communication and querying user interface properties), and employing hooking techniques.

Screen readers are essential to people who are blind and are helpful to people who are visually impaired, illiterate, have learning, or motor disability.

Some screen readers can read the text in more than one language, provided that the language of the material is encoded in its metadata.

Some screen reading programs like VoiceOver have language verbosity settings. For example, if a user visits website based in the United Kingdom, the text would be read with an English accent, and when the US-based, it would be read in an American accent.
