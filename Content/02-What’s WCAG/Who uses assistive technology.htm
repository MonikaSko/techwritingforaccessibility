Who uses assistive technology?

This section tells you why you should create accessible documentation and for whom it is useful.

When creating technical documentation, remember that a significant proportion of your audience may have learning difficulties or some kind of disabilities (temporary or permanent).

Developing accessible content should start with thinking about HOW users access your content and engage with it visually as well as audibly.

Taking into consideration the type of disability, we can distinguish:

  1. Temporal disabilities

  2. Social and situational disabilities

    1. Technical problems such as slow or intermittent internet connections or differences in hardware quality.

    2. Environmental restrictions, such as imposed silence (in the case of shared workrooms or a library), poor lighting, bright sunlight, or noise.

    3. People with different language levels in which you create the content (if it is English, for some users, it may be the second or another language they speak).

  3. Permanent disabilities

    1. Different vision abilities

      1. People with low vision most often use screen magnification software that zooms on a selected fragment of a website.

      2. Blind people who use screen readers - programs that read text aloud. They use a mouse or touchpad very rarely but rely on the keyboard instead.

    2. Different motor abilities

      1. People with reduced mobility that use voice-activated or eye-tracking software, as well as head and mouth pointers.

    3. Different hearing abilities

      1. Users with hearing impairments that use subtitles and transcription to access video and audio materials.

    4. Different cognitive abilities

      1. Difficulty with sustaining concentration, short-term/working memory (limited storage), ability to manipulate objects, ability to generate verbal output, process incoming visual stimuli, and enable goal-oriented behavior (planning, executing goals).

Web accessibility can be divided based on its nature. When creating web pages, it is crucial for the whole product team to cooperate and combine their efforts. From web developers and designer staking care of more technical aspects of creating a page like fonts, colors, page layouts, contrast ratios, form labels, providing keyboard accessibility, etc., to people creating content like UX and technical writers who should create accessible content as soon as the content management talks and planning starts.
